Infinite, endless, black.
The kind of darkness that would be described as having weight, if weight could be felt in this void.
There is no heat, no warmth, no cold, no smells, no sound.

Do I even exist?

Am I just a mote, floating in nothing?
But what are these thoughts I'm having?
There must be something because otherwise, there would be no voice in my head, no words to describe this emptiness.
Feeling nothing is... comfortable.
Hold onto the feeling that may or may not exist.
When light returns, it will bring pain.
Awareness of self.
I am nothing, inside of nothing, in this space that may not exist.
And that's just how I like it.

Without warning, without pretense, pain blossoms inside of my head.
Wait, my head?
I can feel again... my eyes are closed.
No!
I don't want this burden of conciousness... let me return to nothing.
But alas, the void has slipped away, I am here, I am alive.

And my head feels like the aftermath of a pitched battle between elephants.

I can sense that there is light, if only I could open my eyes to see it.
I am lying on a mattress in a space within a building, but where am I?
Am I alone?
Why is my throat so dry?
I try to swallow, but I might as well be attempting to gulp down a mug of flour.
I realise... that my head is in the middle of something foul-smelling, wet and sticky.

Oh, please let me return to infinite, unfeeling dark!

Reality, I reject you and this shame!

With difficulty, I open my eyes... and raise my head.
On the floor, multiple empty bottles... the air is thick with the cloying scent of alcohol mixed with vomit.
The room rolls and spins, unfamiliar to me through the haze.
But the more pressing matter remains, who even am I?

Must of been quite the night.
Guess I'll never drink again.
The lies we tell ourselves.
