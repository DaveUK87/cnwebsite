Wings spread, the white gull soars through a blanket of cerulean blue, carried on currents of wind that whip and thrash about as though angry at their own aimlessness.
But the gull is a master of the skies, effortlessly she glides ever onwards over thick green canopies and rolling, crashing waves of deep, dark navy.
Where she travels, can only be guessed at, why she flies can only be admired, a statement of nature - "look upon my work and be awed."

The lowly earthworm does not look, he is unconcerned with such grand, sweeping notions.
To him, he lives in a dank, cold and dark world.
His only concerns are feeding, mating and living another day.
At any moment, the gull could swoop from her kingdom in the clouds and end the earthworm's existence.
Such is life, such is nature.
We live, we try to be as free as a white gull, but how many of us are earthworms?
Just getting by, to live another day.

At night, I soar and screech in exultation, the whole world my playground.
Then I wake and in the cold light of the morning, I am the earthworm.
